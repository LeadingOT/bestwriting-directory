# What is Content Brief

**A content brief is a strategic document that provides a writer or content creator with all the essential instructions, guidelines, and reference materials needed to produce a piece of content that meets specific business and editorial goals.**

A content brief serves as the foundational blueprint for any content project, bridging the gap between a content strategist's vision and the final written piece. It moves beyond a simple topic assignment by detailing the target audience, primary objective (e.g., to inform, convert, or rank for specific keywords), core message, and key points that must be covered. This document ensures alignment from the outset, reducing ambiguity and the need for extensive revisions.

Typically, a comprehensive brief includes several core components: the working title, target keyword(s) and search intent, a content outline or structure, competitor or reference links, style and tone guidelines, word count, internal linking suggestions, and a clear call-to-action. It may also specify technical requirements, such as meta descriptions or image alt-text instructions. By consolidating these elements, the brief acts as a single source of truth for everyone involved in the content creation process.

**Why it matters / Use cases**
A well-crafted content brief is critical for efficiency, consistency, and quality. It streamlines workflows by giving writers clear direction, which speeds up the drafting process and minimizes back-and-forth communication. For businesses, it ensures content consistently supports SEO strategy and brand voice across all writers and platforms. Practically, a brief is used when outsourcing work to freelance writers, directing an in-house team, or managing a content calendar—any scenario where clear, replicable instructions are needed to produce on-brand, goal-oriented content. For example, a brief for a blog post titled "Best Running Shoes for Flat Feet" would specify the target keyword, instruct the writer to include product comparisons from three listed brands, adopt a helpful and expert tone, and link to a specific buying guide page on the company's site.
